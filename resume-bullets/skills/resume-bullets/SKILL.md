# Resume Bullets

Convert work history into sharp, drop-in bullets. Focus on wording, not resume layout or formatting.

## Modes

- **Generate:** Turn an unstructured account into bullets.
- **Review / revise:** Check existing bullets against these rules. Report issues for a review request; rewrite for a fix request. When new material overlaps a prior bullet, merge it into the stronger version rather than duplicating the accomplishment.

## Write strong, honest bullets

- Lead with the result, then say what was done and how: `[impact] by [action and method]`. Prefer a strong past-tense verb (for example, Cut, Led, Shipped, Designed, Automated, Grew); use present tense only for ongoing current work.
- Keep bullets under about 30 words. Give each distinct accomplishment one bullet; do not split the same win across bullets.
- Lead each role with its 2–3 strongest items. Drop routine work—bug fixes, reviews, meetings—unless it produced a distinct result; otherwise fold it into a stronger bullet. If it must remain, note it as a candidate to cut.
- Surface provided measures such as revenue, users, latency, percentage change, time saved, team size, scale, QPS, rows, or dollars. Never invent a number or an impact claim.
- If a real, described impact lacks a useful number, use a clear placeholder such as `[X%]` or `[$X]` and briefly ask the user to verify it. Use no more than 2–3 placeholders, only where they matter most. Unless the user requests coaching or questions, do not pause to interview them.
- Treat prototypes, proposals, and unshipped POCs as targeted or demonstrated outcomes, not achieved ones. It is valid to claim a pilot, award, leadership buy-in, or projection when described. Flag ambiguous measures (for example, “raised attendance 80%”) and unverified inferred impact.

## Make the accomplishment clear

- Write for a skimming recruiter or HR screener. Use plain language, expand non-universal acronyms on first use, and replace internal names, codenames, and jargon.
- Extract the achievement implied by casual wording: unclaimed ownership may show initiative; onboarding help may show mentorship. Do not merely transcribe the brain dump.

## Emphasize by role

- **Software Engineer:** Show technical difficulty, scale, concrete technologies or systems, and measurable results.
- **Program / Product Manager:** Lead with planning, design, coordination, and initiative; include only enough technical detail for credibility. Elevate self-directed projects, tools, communities, events, teaching, mentoring, and training, even if outside the job section.
- **Other roles:** Prioritize outcomes, ownership, initiative, and available scale over implementation detail.

## Output

Return grouped bullet lists only. Use light labels for roles, companies, projects, or `Notes`.

- **Generate:** Put polished bullets under the relevant groups.
- **Review / revise:** Put rewritten bullets first. Add `Notes` only for brief explanations of changes, placeholders, ambiguity, unverified claims, or candidate-to-cut bullets.

Do not add prose summaries, resume layouts, or full-document formatting.
