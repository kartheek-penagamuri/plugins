# Craft Software Resume

Turn truthful career evidence into a targeted resume that a recruiter can understand in a quick skim. Do not invent metrics, scope, technologies, or outcomes.

## Workflow

1. Identify the target role, level, company/job description, geography, and resume constraint. Ask only for information that materially changes the result.
2. Build an evidence inventory before drafting: roles, accomplishments, projects, technologies actually usable in an interview, initiatives, leadership, teaching, and relevant side work. Class, personal, and unfinished projects can qualify when they strengthen the case.
3. Select evidence for the target role; do not merely copy a chronological work history. Expand relevant material and trim truthful but low-value detail.
4. Draft, then audit the result using the checks below. Return the revised resume, a focused critique, or both—match the user’s request.

## Select the Signal

### Software engineering

- Prioritize technically substantive problems, system scale, engineering judgment, delivery, and independent initiative.
- Include internships and, especially early in a career, roughly three to four substantive projects when work experience alone does not establish practical ability.
- Tailor project selection to the employer’s work: emphasize web and scalable-system work for server-focused roles; emphasize client work for client-product roles.
- List programming languages only at a level the candidate can defend. Separate current proficiency from prior exposure; do not combine distinct languages into a single label.

### Program, product, and engineering management

- Establish technical credibility, then emphasize planning, prioritization, design judgment, initiative, delivery, and cross-functional influence.
- Prefer evidence of roadmaps, dependencies, requirements, trade-offs, launches, stakeholder alignment, and measurable outcomes over implementation detail that does not support the role.
- Treat credible leadership outside a formal PM title—teaching, mentoring, organizing, or leading projects—as usable evidence of communication and leadership.

## Write for a Fast Scan

- Use a clean, consistent, space-efficient layout. For a recent graduate, target one page; use a second page only when the relevant experience warrants it.
- Keep bullets to one or two lines where possible. Use action-led fragments rather than paragraph-like prose.
- Make each bullet answer: what did the candidate do, in what meaningful context, and what changed? Favor this pattern: **action + scope/problem + method + result**.
- Replace responsibilities with accomplishments. Quantify impact, scale, time, quality, cost, adoption, reliability, or revenue when the evidence supports it.
- Remove unsupported traits such as “excellent team player.” Show the trait through a concrete result instead.

## Final Checks

- Confirm the top third of the resume communicates the intended role and strongest evidence within about 15 seconds.
- Verify every fact, metric, technology, date, title, link, and contact detail. Proofread and obtain a critical second review.
- Remove irrelevant personal information for U.S. applications, including age, marital status, and gender.
- Name the submitted file with the candidate’s name and role, not a generic filename.
